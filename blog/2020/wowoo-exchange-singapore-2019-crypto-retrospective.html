Cryptocurrency retrospective: 2019 in review

CRYPTOCURRENCY IN 2019 AT A GLANCE

On a macro level, the year was marked by global debt levels rising and political instability, with protestors taking to the streets fighting political and economic inequality. Further, technology companies started to make further inroads into financial services.

All three of these factors can be seen as wider reasons for a general crypto asset market recovery in 2019, a stark contrast to crypto prices in 2018. Bitcoin continued its journey from value store to a safe haven asset. Ethereum held its position as the leading smart contract platform, with Decentralized Finance emerging as the strongest use case.

Gaming and developer tools (such as node infrastructure and data analytics) continued to be a focus for many and grew substantially. Finally, prime brokerage stacks continued to be developed, with the likes of exchange, custody and execution services being tackled

If there was one defying continued movement in crypto assets during the year, it was the continued acceptance (and acquisition) of cryptocurrency from three key segments:

1. Retail users: in general, digital assets continue to resonate with millennials.

2. Institutions: financial incumbents and technology companies introduced products to meet both retail and institutional demand. Samsung and HTC both have introduced phones with built in Cryptocurrency wallets, and we can't go without mentioning Facebook’s Libra project, that was probably the most eye-catching. However, the launch of ICE's Bakkt, alongside CME's existing offerings, means we now have fully regulated bitcoin futures products, from two of the most established exchanges, and covering both physical and cash settlement. Add to this Fidelity growing its custody offering for crypto assets, we now have a regular stream of announcements from established, reputable financial firms establishing a presence within the cryptocurrency ecosystem, a sure sign of the industry maturing - headlines that would previously have caused a euphoric wave of excitement and the accompanying price action, now just act as an additional piece of the puzzle in place.

3. Nation states: began exploring the concept of central bank digital currencies (CBDC). On a global level, many nations leaned into the regulatory environment proposing new regulations and laws. Countries are at different stages of "First they ignore you, then they laugh at you, then they fight you, then you win".
